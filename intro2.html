Memory/RAM

RAM is a form of computer memory and usually comes in pairs. The pairs of sticks go onto the motherboard. The larger and faster the RAM then the more applications and sites you will be able to run.

CPU Cooler

The CPU cooler is very simple. It is either a fan or some form of liquid cooling that is designed to cool the CPU and keep it from over heating.

GPU

The GPU, also known as a graphics card, is what really seperates a gaming pc from a gaming console. These high end cards are what provide the amazing graphics and frame rate on PC video games. High end gpus can reach up to 4k graphics and fps(frames per second) rates ranging from 100-400 fps, far more than any console.

Storage

Storage is what allows you to contain any apps, media, files, etc. There are two types of storages drives you can get. Solid State Drives and Hard Drives. Solid State Drives are newer and faster storage drives and some even go on the motherboard. Hard Drives usually require a sata cable for connection.

Power Supply

To get every part on the computer up and running you will need a power supply. Everything connects to the power supply, which connects to the power outlet.
